Nd residence time (600-1000 years) is shorter than ocean mixing time (~1,500 years).

üìä Interpreting ŒµNd Values

  • ŒµNd = 0: Same as bulk Earth (chondrite)
  • ŒµNd > 0 (positive): More radiogenic than bulk Earth ‚Üí mantle-derived
  • ŒµNd < 0 (negative): Less radiogenic than bulk Earth ‚Üí continental crust

3. Crustal vs. Mantle Reservoirs

The key to understanding ŒµNd is the fractionation of Sm and Nd during partial melting.

Figure: During partial melting, Nd preferentially enters the melt while Sm stays in the residue. This creates different Sm/Nd ratios between crust and mantle.
Figure: ŒµNd as a tracer of ocean circulation through deep time. Continental margins with old crust contribute negative (non-radiogenic) ŒµNd values, while volcanic arcs contribute positive (radiogenic) values, allowing water mass tracking across ocean basins.

REE Behavior During Melting

Samarium (Sm) and Neodymium (Nd) are both Rare Earth Elements (REE):

  • All REE have 3+ charge
  • Ionic radii decrease with increasing atomic number
  • All REE are "incompatible" (prefer the melt)
  • Light REE (including Nd) are MORE incompatible than heavy REE

üîë The Critical Insight

Nd is less compatible in the mantle than Sm. During melting:

  • Melts become enriched in Nd ‚Üí Low Sm/Nd
  • Mantle residue retains more Sm ‚Üí High Sm/Nd

Over billions of years, the high Sm/Nd mantle produces more ¬π‚Å¥¬≥Nd from ¬π‚Å¥‚Å∑Sm decay ‚Üí higher ŒµNd

Case Study: Tectonic Gateway Changes

Figure: ŒµNd evolution during the opening of Drake Passage (Eocene). The increase in ŒµNd values in the Atlantic sector of the Southern Ocean indicates increasing Pacific water mass influence, marking the establishment of the Antarctic Circumpolar Current (ACC). After Scher & Martin (2006).

üåä Panama Closure (~3 Ma)

The closure of the Central American Seaway fundamentally changed Atlantic circulation:

  • Before closure: Pacific water mixed into Caribbean ‚Üí higher ŒµNd
  • After closure: Atlantic isolated ‚Üí ŒµNd became more negative
  • Enhanced thermohaline circulation ‚Üí Gulf Stream intensification
  • Contributed to Northern Hemisphere glaciation

üßä Glacial-Interglacial Changes

ŒµNd records show circulation changes during ice ages:

  • Glacial periods: Reduced NADW production ‚Üí less negative ŒµNd in deep Atlantic
  • Interglacials: Strong NADW ‚Üí more negative ŒµNd
  • This reflects changing dominance of northern vs. southern water masses

  • Turbidites: Trace submarine flows to source regions
  • Ice-rafted debris: Track iceberg calving sources

üìù Key Points Summary

  • ŒµNd notation expresses ¬π‚Å¥¬≥Nd/¬π‚Å¥‚Å¥Nd relative to bulk Earth (CHUR)

  • Atlantic deep water is unradiogenic (ŒµNd ‚âà -13), Pacific is more radiogenic (ŒµNd ‚âà -4)
